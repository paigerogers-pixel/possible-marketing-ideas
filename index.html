The Core Belief

People rarely outperform their self-image. Behavior follows identity, not instruction.

Our marketing shapes identity through truth and standards. We are not telling people what to do. We are helping them become the kind of person who behaves differently.

Four Non-Negotiables

  • Marketing does not persuade — Marketing increases desirability
  • Marketing does not reassure — Marketing filters for the right person
  • We do not chase engagement — We post when it deserves to be posted
  • We do not react to news — We remain calm under pressure

The Enemy

An industry optimised for engagement, not outcomes.

Social Platforms

Optimised for engagement — hot takes beat patient thinking.

Marketing Agencies

Optimised for activity — more campaigns = more revenue, regardless of results.

Growth Tactics

Optimised for attention — viral beats honest, urgency beats truth.

We do not attack competitors by name. We attack the system that produces bad marketers at scale.

The Review Ritual

Before anything ships, the owner must answer the following. If answers feel hand-wavy, it is not ready.

  1. What belief does this reinforce?
  2. What behavior does this encourage?
  3. Who does this intentionally filter out?
  4. What temptation did we resist by shipping this?
  5. Would this still exist if metrics disappeared?

Metrics That Matter

What to track, and what to ignore.

Discard These

  • Impressions
  • Engagement rates
  • Follower counts
  • Click-through rates
  • Viral posts
  • Short-term conversions

Track These

  • Organic referrals (quality over volume)
  • Behavior change in audience
  • Long-term retention
  • Thoughtful questions in comments
  • People who quote your language
  • New audience acquisition cost

Cult growth is slow — until it isn't. Look for quiet referrals, thoughtful engagement, and long-term retention. The strongest sign this is working: people quote your language back to you.
